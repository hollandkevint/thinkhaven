# How to Use AI
*By Dan Hock*
Source: https://www.danhock.co/p/how-to-use-ai

## Summary

Hock argues that most people use AI badly because they treat it as a search engine or a generator. The high-leverage pattern is **structured exploration**: long working sessions where the human supplies judgment and constraints, the model surfaces options and challenges, and the artifact evolves through hundreds of exchanges. He describes a "valley of despair" most users hit — the point where AI output looks plausible but is shallow, and most people quit there instead of pushing through to real depth. The users who get past it are Early Adopters willing to do the work of structured prompting, context management, and critique.

## Mapping to Thinkhaven

Thinkhaven productizes the workflow Hock describes, instead of leaving it as a discipline each user has to invent for themselves.

**1. The three modes are structured exploration.** Assessment, Stress Test, and Executive Prep all share Hock's core pattern: bounded scope, persistent context, deliberate sequencing of question types, artifacts that evolve across turns. The product opinionates the structure so users don't have to design the session themselves.

**2. No-Man mode is the valley-of-despair solution.** Hock's central failure mode is users abandoning AI when output gets shallow or agreeable. No-Man mode forces the conversation through complexity instead of letting the user route around it — the AI's job is to push back, not to please. This is the anti-sycophancy positioning made operational.

**3. Executive Prep productizes the thought-partner workflow.** Hock describes 300+ exchange cycles, context compactions, and drafts that evolve through repeated critique. Executive Prep gives that workflow a container: a session, a persistent artifact, structured critique passes, and outputs designed to survive a stakeholder room. The user doesn't have to know to compact context or sequence critiques — the mode does it.

**4. Thinkhaven targets Hock's Early Adopters.** The users who get value from his approach are the same ones who will pay for Thinkhaven: people who already believe AI can be a thought partner, who are frustrated with shallow output, and who will invest the time to get past the valley. Both Solo Entrepreneurs (Assessment) and Executives/Product Leaders (Stress Test, Executive Prep) sit in this segment — they differ in willingness to pay, not in disposition toward the tool.

## Implication for the roadmap

If Hock is right, the product's job is to *lower the activation cost* of structured exploration — not to teach the discipline, but to remove the steps where users currently fail. Mode design, default prompts, and challenge sequencing should be evaluated against that bar.
